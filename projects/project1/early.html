Early Years:

By 1998, “Backrub” was renamed to “Google” after “googol (a mathematical term for the number one followed by 100 zeroes)” (Hosch) and had begun to gain some attention by investors. With $100,000 from Andy Bechtolsheim, Sun Microsystems co-founder, “Google Inc. was officially born” (Google).

Following the creation of the corporation, Google’s new homebase was a garage owned by Susan Wojcicki. Brin and Page continued to gain funding, further develop Google, and process more and more searches each day. Over the next couple of years with the growth, expansion, and overall increase of content on the internet, Page and Brin faced the challenge of gathering and storing this increase of data efficiently.

As more and more people started using the internet, more data was being added throughout the internet, and more and more search queries were going to Google each day. Brin and Page state they designed Google to “scale well to extremely large data sets” (Page and Brin), meaning the “PageRank” algorithm was improved to become more efficient, with the help of cost per performance decreasing with the advancement of technology.
