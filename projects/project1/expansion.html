Expansion:

In 2001, Larry Page stepped down as CEO to become “president of products” (Encyclopædia Britannica) and was replaced by Eric Schmidt to work on projects like Gmail and Google Docs, although Brin, Page, and Schmidt worked together to continue running the company.

In 2002, Yahoo! failed to “acquire Google for $3 billion” (The Verge). By the early 2000s, Google was being used “200 million times a day” (Encyclopædia Britannica), Google continued to receive money from investors and expand.

In 2004, Google had over one-thousand employees and leased buildings in Silicon Valley, which are now known as Googleplex. Google continued to expand and work on many projects, to become what we know it is today

It is amazing to see how Sergey Brin and Larry Page started Google as a project in grad school and is now one of the largest companies on the planet. Brin and Page’s creation forever changed the internet and influenced how we operate on the daily. Google and their many products are used by millions all over the world.
